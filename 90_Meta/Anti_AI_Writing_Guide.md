Fake Transitions to Kill
AI loves connecting paragraphs with transitions that sound smooth but say nothing: "That said..." "That
being said..." "With that in mind..." "Interestingly enough..." "It's worth noting that..." "Here's where it gets
interesting..." "But here's the thing..." (when nothing surprising follows)
The fix: If two paragraphs need a bridge, make the bridge carry actual information.
Fake Empathy to Kill
AI's attempt at sounding human often lands as condescending: "We've all been there." "If you're like most
people..." "And that's completely okay." "Give yourself grace." "And that's a beautiful thing."
The fix: Real empathy comes from specificity. Describe the specific situation instead of using a generic
phrase.

## SECTION 2: BANNED STRUCTURES
## The Mirror Opening
The pattern: AI restates your prompt as the first sentence. "Write a post about hiring" becomes "Hiring
was one of the best decisions I made."
The fix: Open with a scene, tension, number, or unexpected angle. Never restate the topic.
The Three-Point Sermon
The pattern: Intro paragraph, Point 1, Point 2, Point 3, conclusion summarizing all three. Every. Single.
## Time.
The fix: Vary your structures. Some posts are stories. Some are one argument with proof. Some are
contrarian takes.
## The Bookend Summary
The pattern: AI always ends by restating what it just said: "In conclusion, by focusing on consistency,
authenticity, and engagement..."
The fix: End with a forward-looking statement, a question, or a CTA. Or just stop.
The Balanced Both-Sides Hedge
The pattern: "While there are certainly challenges, there are also incredible opportunities." AI hedges
everything.
The fix: Have an actual opinion. Real humans have positions. AI has "balance."
The Question-Then-Answer
The pattern: "What's the secret to growth? It's consistency." AI asks and answers in back-to-back lines.
The fix: If you ask a question, let it breathe. Give context before the answer.
## The Ascending Excitement
The pattern: Bullet points always escalate: "First... Second... And most importantly...!"
The fix: Put your strongest point wherever it naturally belongs — sometimes that's the middle.
## SECTION 3: BANNED FORMATTING HABITS

•Excessive bold: AI bolds every key phrase. Rule: max 1–2 bold elements per post.
•Emoji overload: AI adds an emoji per line to seem casual. Rule: only use emojis if YOU actually use
them.
•The list-of-5-or-7 default: AI always picks 5, 7, or 10 items. Rule: use the real number. Prime
numbers (3, 7, 11, 13) feel curated.
•Hashtag block at the end: Mostly useless on LinkedIn now. Max 1–2 specific hashtags.
## SECTION 4: TONE TRAPS
•Toxic positivity: "Every setback is a setup for a comeback!" — Be honest. If something sucked, say
it.
•The expert lecture voice: "There are three fundamental principles..." — Share what you learned,
don't lecture.
•The inspirational close: "The future belongs to those who dare to dream" — End with something
specific to YOUR story.
•The humble brag: "I never expected this, but..." — Share wins directly and honestly.

## THE 7-POINT SNIFF TEST
Before publishing ANY AI-assisted content, run it through these questions:
•1. Could anyone have written this? If yes, add your specific story, numbers, or angle.
•2. Any banned phrases? Search for them. Replace or delete.
•3. Does it open by restating the topic? Rewrite the opening.
•4. Does it end with a summary or inspirational quote? Cut the last paragraph.
•5. Is every claim backed by a specific number from your Fact Dossier? If not, add or remove.
•6. Would your team recognize this as YOUR voice? Check against Voice Profile.
•7. Would you say this out loud to a friend? Read aloud. Rewrite anything that sounds like a
brochure.

## 10 COMMANDMENTS OF ANTI-AI WRITING
•1. Never open by restating the topic. Open with a scene, a number, or a tension.
•2. Never close by summarizing what you just said. End forward, not backward.
•3. Never use a phrase from the Banned Phrases list. Memorize it. Kill on sight.
•4. Never make a claim without a specific number or example. Vague = AI. Specific = human.
•5. Never use more than 2 bold elements per post. Emphasis loses power when overused.
•6. Never write something "anyone" could have written. Your edge is YOUR story.
•7. Never hedge with "both sides" unless you genuinely see both sides. Have an opinion.
•8. Never use fake transitions. Make bridges carry information.
•9. Never end with a motivational quote. End with a real thought or question.
•10. Always read it out loud before publishing. If you wouldn't say it to a friend, don't post it.
