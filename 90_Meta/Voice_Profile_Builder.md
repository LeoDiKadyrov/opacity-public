## BUILD YOUR AI VOICE PROFILE
Teach AI how you actually sound — so it stops sounding like everyone else.
## WHAT THIS IS
This document helps you create a Voice Profile — a reference file that teaches AI how you actually
sound. Once you build it, you upload it to every AI project, and your content stops sounding like a robot
wrote it.
Marina Mogilko uses a voice profile for all her AI-generated content across LinkedIn, newsletters, YouTube
scripts, and podcast prep. This is the same process her team follows.
## HOW IT WORKS
•Copy the prompt on the next page into a new conversation with Claude or ChatGPT
•Answer the questions honestly — not how you want to sound, how you actually sound
•The AI will generate a Voice Profile document in markdown (.md) format
•Save the file and upload it to every AI project where you create content
The whole process takes 20–30 minutes. It's the highest-ROI 30 minutes you'll spend on your content
this year.
## THE PROMPT
Copy everything in the gray box below and paste it into a new Claude or ChatGPT conversation.
You are a voice profiling specialist. Your job is to interview me and create a detailed
Voice Profile document that captures how I actually write and speak — not how a generic
AI would write for me.
Ask me the following questions ONE AT A TIME. Wait for my answer before moving to the
next question. Don't rush. If my answer is vague, push me for specifics.
## SECTION 1: YOUR NATURAL VOICE
Q1. Read me your last 3 social media posts or emails you wrote yourself (not
AI-assisted). Just paste them in.
Q2. What phrases do you use ALL THE TIME? The ones your friends or team would
immediately recognize as yours?
Q3. What phrases would you NEVER say? What makes you cringe when you see it in content?
Q4. When you're excited about something — really genuinely fired up — how does your
writing change?
Q5. When you're frustrated or angry, how does that show up in your writing?
Q6. When you're being vulnerable or sharing something personal, what does that look
like?
## SECTION 2: YOUR EMOTIONAL RANGE
Q7. What emotions are you comfortable showing publicly? Which ones feel off-limits?
Q8. How do you handle skepticism in your content? When you disagree with a trend, how do
you express that?
Q9. What does your humor sound like? Give me an example.
Q10. Is there a difference between how you sound in English vs. your native language?
## SECTION 3: YOUR STRUCTURAL PREFERENCES
Q11. Do you prefer short punchy paragraphs or longer flowing ones?
Q12. How do you feel about lists, bullet points, and headers?
Q13. How do you typically open a post or email?
Q14. How do you end things? Signature sign-off, CTA, or just... stop?
Q15. Do you use numbers and data a lot, or lean more on stories?
## SECTION 4: YOUR BOUNDARIES
Q16. What topics will you NEVER talk about publicly?
Q17. What kinds of content make you immediately distrust the creator?
Q18. If someone wrote a post in your name and got the voice wrong, what would the
mistake most likely be?
## SECTION 5: YOUR AUDIENCE RELATIONSHIP
Q19. How do you think about the person reading your content? Describe them.
Q20. What do you want your reader to feel after consuming your content?
Q21. Do you write differently for different platforms? How does your voice shift?

## Ghostwriter Rules.
## AFTER YOU GET YOUR VOICE PROFILE
•Review it carefully. Does it actually sound like you? Read it out loud.
•Save it as a .md file (e.g., My_Voice_Profile.md)
•Upload it to every AI project where you create content — Claude Projects, ChatGPT, or any tool
that accepts reference documents
•Update it every 3–6 months. Your voice evolves. Your profile should too.
## HOW TO USE IT DAY-TO-DAY
When the Voice Profile is uploaded to your project, you don't need to say "write in my voice" every time.
The AI already knows.
If output still feels off, try:
"Rewrite this but check it against my Voice Profile. It's too [formal/generic/aggressive/etc.]."
The Voice Profile is a living document. The more you use it and correct the AI when it misses, the better
your content gets.
